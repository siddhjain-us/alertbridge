# Natural hazard types (reference)

Short reference for demos and messaging context. Full content also appears in the AlertBridge dashboard “Hazards” card with a link here.

## Floods

Often caused by heavy rain or overflowing rivers, these are very common, with just 6 inches of water able to knock a person down.

## Hurricanes / Tropical cyclones

Intense storms with high-speed winds (over 111 mph for major hurricanes) that cause massive coastal damage through wind and storm surge.

## Earthquakes

Sudden, violent shifts in the Earth's crust that can damage buildings and trigger other disasters like tsunamis or landslides.

## Wildfires

Destructive fires in vegetated areas, frequently ignited by lightning or human action, which are becoming more common due to dry conditions.

## Tornadoes

Rapidly rotating columns of air that can destroy buildings and cause significant casualties.

## Droughts

Long-term dry periods that can destroy crops, cause water shortages, and trigger wildfires.

## Tsunamis

Massive ocean waves, often caused by underwater earthquakes, that can reach heights of up to 100 feet.

## Landslides

The movement of rock, earth, or debris down a slope, often triggered by heavy rain or earthquakes.

## Volcanic eruptions

The release of lava, ash, and gases from a volcano, causing local devastation.
